Layer 1 Feature Detectors
The layer 1 (conv1) kernels are 3x3 feature detectors sensitive to color, oriented edges, or some combination of the two. You can measure the responses of feature detectors by holding up various stimuli to the camera.

Color detection:

  • Use a color palette such as the rainbow figure below to measure each kernel's reponse to color.


  • Notice that kernel 11, labeled the "Red patch detector", also responds to violet, because violet is a mixture of blue and red. How accurate are the kernel color descriptions? Test them and see.

  • Which kernel responds best to light-colored skin? Which responds best to dark-colored skin? Which responds well to lips?

Orientation detection: Some kernels look for horizontal, vertical, or diagonal edges. For example, kernel 8 is a horizontal edge detector that looks for a row of light pixels above a row of dark pixels. It doesn't care about color, so the R, G, and B channels of kernel 8, each of which has a 3x3 pattern of weights, are similar: the top row has positive weights and the bottom row has negative weights. If shown a black square on a white background, kernel 8 will respond strongly to the top edge of the square. Kernel 4 is a little messier but is also basically a horizontal edge detector, with roughly opposite weights to kernel 8. Kernel 4 will respond strongly to the bottom edge of the square.
